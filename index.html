Wastewater Tunnel Project

Auckland, NZ

Back to Projects

Project Overview

The Central Interceptor is New Zealand's largest wastewater tunnel project, consisting of a 14.7 km main tunnel, multiple shafts, a major pump station, and substantial wastewater network infrastructure. Our team contributed to various phases of design, such as preliminary design (PD) and detailed design (DD), addressed design change requests (DCR), and assisted with construction phase support (CPS).

We were involved in the modelling, analysis, and design of temporary works for control chambers and pump stations, temporary and permanent works for shaft lining and manhole structures, and the design of box culverts and retaining structures. Our team also drove end-to-end procurement, commercial leadership, financial management, cost-optimization, schedule management, and complex claims negotiation.

Underground Retaining Structures

India, New Zealand, Australia

Back to Projects

Project Overview

A critical water main rupture occurred along a mountainous road in NSW after retained slopes along Jenolan Caves Road at Hampton failed during heavy rainfall. The failure prompted comprehensive geotechnical and structural investigations to assess the damaged slopes and design robust remediation solutions.

Our team was involved in the design and internal peer review of the piled barrier retaining wall structure, ensuring structural stability and longevity. The remediation design incorporated advanced soil mechanics analysis and modern construction techniques to prevent future failures and ensure road safety.

Footbridges and Vehicular Bridges

Hong Kong

Back to Projects

Project Overview

This project involved the design of link bridges connecting the Amoy Plaza and the East Kowloon Cultural Centre (EKCC) across Ngau Tau Kok Road and Highway Flyover no. K56. The Amoy Plaza footbridge structure consists of a steel truss bridge designed to facilitate pedestrian connectivity and enhance urban accessibility in this dynamic cultural district.

Additionally, the redevelopment of Siu Ho Depot provides infrastructure and enabling works for future residential and public facilities. Our team was involved in the design of steel structures and steel connections for five link bridges (footbridges). In addition, our team handled the modelling and analysis of the carriage bridge at Tai Ho Interchange and prepared the necessary design calculations and reports for all structural elements.

Bridge Structural Investigation

Scotland

Back to Projects

Project Overview

This project involved the structural assessment of a historic bridge in Scotland that required a comprehensive review of traffic load models for UK highway structures, considering heavier vehicles (SV80, ALL 40T, and Piling Rig loads). The assessment aimed to evaluate the bridge's capacity to handle modern traffic patterns and increased vehicle weights.

Our team was involved in modelling and analysing these additional bridge loads and assessing the capacity of the bridge's structural elements to handle increased loading. We provided detailed recommendations for maintenance, reinforcement, or load restrictions to ensure continued safe operation of this heritage structure while accommodating modern infrastructure requirements.
